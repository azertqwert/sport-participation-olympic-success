Sport Participation and Olympic Success

Exploring the relationship between access to sport and Olympic performance

Introduction

Sport plays an important social role by contributing to public health, social inclusion, and individual well-being. However, access to sport and opportunities to reach elite performance are not evenly distributed across countries. While some nations achieve strong international success, others face structural limitations related to resources, infrastructure, or participation levels. This project aims to explore the relationship between sport participation rates and Olympic success across countries. By examining these two dimensions together, the project seeks to better understand whether broad access to sport is associated with higher levels of elite performance, or whether Olympic success is mainly driven by other factors. More broadly, this analysis contributes to discussions on inequality and fairness in access to sport at an international level.

To investigate this relationship, the project relies on openly accessible datasets related to sport participation and Olympic medals. These datasets are cleaned, harmonized, and aggregated in order to allow comparison across countries. Sport participation rates are used as an indicator of access to sport, while the number of Olympic medals serves as a proxy for elite sporting success. The analysis is structured around the following research questions: Is it possible to meaningfully compare sport participation rates across countries? Is Olympic success evenly distributed among countries? Is there a correlation between sport participation rates and Olympic medal counts?
